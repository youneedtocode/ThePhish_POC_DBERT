Welcome to ThePhish!

ThePhish is an AI-powered phishing email detection tool that analyzes suspicious emails and classifies them as malicious or safe using a fine-tuned DistilBERT model.

To use ThePhish, the analyst can click on the "List emails" button at the top of the page and select an email to analyze from the table.

Each email is processed using deep learning techniques, and the tool presents classification results with phishing probability scores, helping the analyst make an informed decision.

The motivation behind ThePhish Project was to augment an open-source phishing analysis tool (ThePhish) with advanced machine learning, specifically DistilBERT, to automatically detect phishing emails with high accuracy. DistilBERT, a lightweight transformer model, can understand email language patterns far better than manual rules, enabling detection of subtle phishing cues in the email body and headers.

The tool has been trained and evaluated on a large, realistic corpus of emails drawn from datasets like Enron, CEAS, SpamAssassin, Nazario, and others. This ensures generalizability to diverse phishing techniques encountered in real-world scenarios.

This version of ThePhish runs independently and does not require integration with external platforms like TheHive, Cortex, or MISP. Analysts can directly view verdicts and supporting metadata within the interface.
